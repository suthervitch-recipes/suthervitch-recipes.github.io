ingredients:
  - 1/2 pound guanciale or pancetta, cut into small cubes
  - 3 large whole eggs plus 2 large yolks
  - 1 cup finely grated Pecorino Romano cheese, plus more for serving
  - 1 cup finely grated Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese
  - 1 pound spaghetti or rigatoni
  - Kosher salt
  - Freshly ground black pepper
notes: |-
  From https://www.seriouseats.com/pasta-carbonara-sauce-recipe

  Do not use cream. The creamy texture comes from emulsifying the rendered pork fat, pasta water, and the egg/cheese mixture. Allow your eggs to come to room temperature before mixing.

1 Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil.

2 In a large skillet or Dutch oven, cook the guanciale over medium-low heat until the fat renders and the meat is crispy, about 10 minutes. Remove from heat.

3 In a medium bowl, whisk together the whole eggs, egg yolks, Pecorino Romano, Parmigiano-Reggiano, and a generous amount of black pepper.

4 Add the pasta to the boiling water and cook until al dente. Reserve at least 1 cup of the starchy pasta water.

5 Drain the pasta and immediately add it to the skillet with the guanciale and rendered fat. Toss to coat off the heat.

6 Slowly whisk 1/4 cup of the hot pasta water into the egg and cheese mixture to temper the eggs.

7 Pour the tempered egg mixture over the pasta in the skillet, tossing vigorously off the heat until a creamy, glossy sauce forms. Add more pasta water a tablespoon at a time if the sauce is too thick.

8 Serve immediately with extra grated cheese and black pepper.
