ingredients:
  - 2 pints cherry tomatoes
  - 8 oz block feta cheese, plus extra crumbled for garnish
  - 1/4 cup + 1 tbsp extra virgin olive oil, divided
  - Salt, to taste
  - Black pepper, to taste
  - 1/2 medium yellow onion, finely chopped
  - Crushed red pepper flakes, to taste
  - 1 (28 oz) can crushed tomatoes with juices
  - 4 cups low sodium vegetable broth
  - 1 tbsp fresh thyme, finely chopped
  - 4 garlic cloves, minced
  - 1/4 cup fresh basil, chopped, plus more for garnish
  - 3/4 cup orzo pasta (or other short pasta)
notes: |-

  #### Original Recipe Notes

  Storage: If making ahead of time, it’s best not to add the orzo until you are ready to serve.

  Without the pasta, the soup will keep refrigerated for 4–5 days and frozen for up to 6 months.

  #### Original Tips

  - Crowd the tomatoes in the baking dish if you want a saucier result.
  - For smoother soup, blend before adding the orzo.
  - For leftovers, cook the orzo separately and add it to bowls when serving.
  - Goat cheese can substitute for feta.
  - Other small pasta shapes (ditalini, shells, etc.) work well.

1 Preheat oven to 400°F.

2 Add cherry tomatoes to an oven-safe baking dish. Pour over 1/4 cup olive oil, season with salt and pepper, and toss.

3 Place feta block in the center of the tomatoes and turn to coat with oil.

4 Bake 35–40 mins until tomatoes burst and feta softens.

5 Meanwhile heat remaining 1 tbsp olive oil in a pot over medium heat.

6 Add onion and cook about 5 mins until soft. Season with salt, pepper, and red pepper flakes.

7 Add garlic, crushed tomatoes, broth, and thyme. Simmer gently.

8 Transfer baked tomatoes and feta into the soup pot.

9 Add chopped basil and stir.

10 Return to simmer, add orzo, and cook 7–8 mins until pasta is tender.

11 Taste and adjust seasoning.

12 Serve topped with extra feta, basil, and black pepper.
