title: Double Chocolate Chip Cookies
description: 'These double chocolate chip cookies are thick, chewy, and packed with rich cocoa and gooey chocolate chips in every single bite.'
portions: 24 cookies
prepTime: 15 mins
cookTime: 10 mins
cover: null
ingredients:
  - 115g (½c) unsalted butter, softened
  - 100g (½c) granulated sugar
  - 110g (½c) packed light brown sugar
  - 1 large egg
  - 5ml (1t) vanilla extract
  - 150g (1¼c) all-purpose flour
  - 40g (½c) unsweetened cocoa powder
  - 3g (½t) baking soda
  - 2g (¼t) salt
  - 180g (1c) semi-sweet chocolate chips
notes: |-
  From https://preppykitchen.com/double-chocolate-chip-cookies/

  #### **Author Notes:**
  - Room temperature butter is crucial. It shouldn't be melted or too cold, just soft enough to hold a fingerprint indentation.
  - If you prefer a flatter, crispier cookie, bake them immediately. For thicker cookies, chill the scooped dough balls in the fridge for 30 minutes before baking.

1 Preheat your oven to 175°C (350°F) and line two large baking sheets with parchment paper.

2 In a stand mixer fitted with the paddle attachment or using a hand mixer, cream together the softened butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar on medium speed for about 2 to 3 minutes until smooth and fluffy.

3 Beat in the egg and vanilla extract until fully incorporated.

4 In a separate medium bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour, unsweetened cocoa powder, baking soda, and salt.

5 Gradually add the dry flour mixture to the wet butter mixture, mixing on low speed just until the flour disappears. Fold in the chocolate chips by hand.

6 Use a cookie scoop to drop 30ml (2T) balls of dough onto the prepared baking sheets, leaving about 5cm (2") of space between each cookie.

7 Bake for 9 to 11 minutes until the edges are set but the centers still look slightly soft. Let sit on the baking sheet for 5 minutes before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely.
