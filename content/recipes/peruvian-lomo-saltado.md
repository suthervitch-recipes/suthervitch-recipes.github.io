ingredients:
  - 2 tablespoons vegetable oil, divided
  - 1 pound sirloin steak, cut into strips
  - Salt, to taste
  - Black pepper, to taste
  - ½ red onion, sliced
  - 1 tomato, medium, sliced
  - 2 cloves garlic, minced
  - 1 tablespoon aji amarillo paste
  - 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  - 1 tablespoon white vinegar
  - 1 tablespoon fresh cilantro, chopped
  - 1 pound french fries, cooked, hot
  - White rice, cooked, for serving (optional)

1 Heat 1 tablespoon of the vegetable oil in a large pan or wok over high heat.

2 Season the sirloin steak strips with salt and pepper to taste. Add the steak to the pan and sear until nicely browned on all sides, about 2–3 minutes. Remove the steak from the pan and set it aside.

3 In the same pan, heat the remaining 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil. Add the sliced red onion and cook for 1–2 minutes until slightly softened.

4 Add the sliced tomato, minced garlic, and aji amarillo paste. Cook for an additional 1 minute.

5 Return the seared steak to the pan along with any resting juices.

6 Pour in the soy sauce and white vinegar. Toss everything together and cook for another 1–2 minutes until the sauce evenly coats the meat and vegetables.

7 Remove the pan from the heat and stir in the chopped fresh cilantro.

8 Gently fold in the hot cooked french fries, or serve the lomo saltado immediately alongside the fries and white rice.
