ingredients:
  - 2 dried ancho chilies, stemmed and seeded
  - 2 dried pasilla chilies, stemmed and seeded
  - 1 quart low-sodium chicken broth, divided
  - 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  - 2 pounds ground beef (or boneless short ribs)
  - 1 large onion, finely chopped
  - 4 cloves garlic, minced
  - 1 tablespoon ground cumin
  - 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  - 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  - 1 (28-ounce) can crushed tomatoes
  - 2 (15-ounce) cans kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  - 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  - 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  - Salt and pepper to taste
notes: |-
  From https://www.seriouseats.com/the-best-chili-recipe

  Using a blend of toasted dried chilies instead of generic chili powder creates a much deeper, more complex flavor profile. The soy sauce and fish sauce act as umami boosters.

1 Toast the dried chilies in a dry skillet over medium-high heat until fragrant, about 2 minutes. Transfer to a blender.

2 Bring 2 cups of the chicken broth to a simmer and pour over the toasted chilies. Let sit for 10 minutes to soften, then blend until perfectly smooth.

3 Heat the vegetable oil in a large Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add the beef and cook until nicely browned. Remove the beef and set aside, leaving the fat in the pot.

4 Lower the heat to medium. Add the chopped onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes. Stir in the garlic, cumin, and oregano, cooking for 1 minute until fragrant.

5 Add the tomato paste and cook for 1 minute, stirring constantly.

6 Pour in the chili puree, the remaining 2 cups of chicken broth, crushed tomatoes, kidney beans, browned beef, soy sauce, and fish sauce.

7 Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover partially and simmer for 1.5 to 2 hours, stirring occasionally until thick and rich.

8 Season with salt and pepper to taste before serving.
