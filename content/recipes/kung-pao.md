Deboned, deskinned chicken thigh, 300g. This’ll be tenderized and cut into \~1/2 inch cubes. You could also use chicken breast, but in my tests they came out a bit dryer and were a bit more of a pain to work with.

Dried chilis, 12, Sichuan Erjingtiao (干二荆条) or Chili de Arbol. Deseeded and cut into \~1/2 inch sections. So the chilis that we’re using here are Sichuan erjingtiao – they’re not a very spicy cultivar (\~20000 scovilles) and have a real nice color to em. For this recipe, feel free to use Arbols, cayennes, or anything that’s red and similarly spicy in the C. Annum or C. Fructesens cultivars.

Sichuan peppercorns (花椒), 15. So the Sichuan peppercorns here are just to balance the heat of the chilis – notice we’re using like an absurdly small amount here. This dish isn’t the ‘numbing spicy’ flavor profile, you really don’t want so many peppercorns that your mouth starts tingling.

Peanuts (花生仁) -or- cashews (腰果), 50g. So the classic here’s gunna be peanuts, but when I make this personally for myself I’ll often find myself reaching for the cashews (mostly because I like cashews, but you also don’t need to peel em which’s nice). We’ll be deep frying these – I’ve seen some people use roasted peanuts as a shortcut which isn’t the same but would be fine in a pinch.

Leek (大葱), \~1/3 stalk. Cut into \~1/2 inch sections. This’s gunna be our veg in the stir-fry.

Ginger (姜), 1 inch. Sliced.

Garlic, 2 cloves. Sliced.

Marinade for the chicken: ½ tsp salt, ½ tsp sugar, \~½ egg white mixed with 1 tsp cornstarch (生粉), 1 tsp liaojiu a.k.a. Shaoxing wine (料酒), ½ tsp dark soy sauce (老抽). So first off, I know that ‘a half an egg white’ is really an annoying amount – feel free to use the whole egg white if ya want. The dark soy sauce here’s gunna give this marinade some color – one very traditional version of Sichuan Kung Pao's to marinate in some fried and caramelized sugar… that’s a royal pain, we’ll just opt for the dark soy sauce instead. This order that you add these ingredients to this marinade is actually quite important to this dish, more on that in the process below.

Sauce for Kung Pao chicken: 2 tbsp sugar, 2 tbsp dark Chinese vinegar (either 陈醋 or 香醋is ok), ½ tbsp liaojiu a.k.a. Shaoxing wine (料酒), ½ tsp light soy sauce (生抽), ¼ tsp dark soy sauce (老抽), ¼ tsp salt, 1/8 tsp chicken boullion powder (鸡粉). All mixed well.

Slurry of 1 tsp cornstarch (生粉) mixed with \~1 tbsp of water. This slurry’s gunna get added in the very end of the stirfrying process.
